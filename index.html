✦ Extra Time Saturday

Niederdorf lanes — evening browsing through the old town's atmospheric cobbled streets. Boutiques, bookshops, and hidden courtyards.

Cabaret Voltaire — birthplace of the Dada art movement. Hugo Ball, Tristan Tzara, and friends launched Dada here in 1916. A pilgrimage for art lovers.

Survival Guide

Zürich Card 72 hr covers all trams, buses, S-Bahn, boats on Lake Zürich, the Uetliberg railway, and over 40 museums — including Kunsthaus and Museum Rietberg.

Activate your card in the ZVV app the moment you land at the airport. It starts counting from activation, not purchase.

Home tram stop: Sihlquai/HB — 3 minutes from the hotel. Trams 4, 11, 13, and 17 all stop here.

Note: Bahnhofquai/HB tram stop is closed until December 2026. Use Sihlquai/HB instead.

Zürich HB (main station) is a 5-minute walk. Airport Express departs every 10 minutes, takes 12 minutes to the airport.

Tipping on transport: Not expected. Swiss public transport runs like clockwork.

Palm Sunday (29 March): Most shops will be closed. Restaurants, museums, and public transport run normally.

Saturday shopping: Bahnhofstrasse shops are open until 6 PM on Saturday. Sprüngli, Globus, and Grieder are all on the main strip.

Souvenirs worth buying: Luxemburgerli from Sprüngli, Swiss chocolate from Läderach, a Victorinox knife from the HB shop.

ZVV App — Zürich Card activation and live tram/train schedules.

SBB Mobile — Swiss Federal Railways. Real-time departures, platform info, connections.

Google Maps — Download offline maps for Zürich AND Copenhagen before departure.

Google Translate — Download offline German pack for restaurant menus and signs.

Rejseplanen — Copenhagen public transport (for the stopover).

Rejsekort / Rejsebillet — Copenhagen metro tickets and travel cards on your phone.

Currency: Swiss Franc (CHF). Denmark uses Danish Krone (DKK). Neither is the Euro.

Cards accepted everywhere. Visa and Mastercard work in virtually every shop and restaurant. Apple Pay and contactless widely accepted.

Tipping: Not required in Switzerland — service is included. Round up or leave 5–10% for exceptional service if you wish.

ATMs: Available at the airport and throughout the city. Avoid currency conversion at the ATM — always choose to be charged in CHF.

eSIM providers: Airalo, Holafly, Saily — install before departure. Best option for reliable data.

US carrier plans: T-Mobile (international included on some plans), AT&T International Day Pass, Verizon TravelPass.

eSIM compatibility: Check your phone supports eSIM before purchasing. iPhone XS and later, most recent Android flagships.

Google Maps: Download offline maps for Zürich AND Copenhagen before departure.

Google Translate: Download offline German pack.

Turn off cellular data when not using Wi-Fi to avoid surprise charges.

Switzerland is NOT in the EU — EU roaming may not apply. Check with your carrier.

Denmark: EU roaming should work with most US carriers' international plans. Verify before departure.

Wi-Fi: Available at both hotels and most cafés/restaurants.

Emergency: 112 works from any phone, even without a SIM card.

Zürich Card fine for traveling without a valid ticket: CHF 90. Always have your card activated.

Trams leave on the exact second — be at the stop early. The Swiss don't wait.

Keep voices low on public transport — Swiss etiquette. Phone calls are frowned upon.

Crossing on red is illegal and will draw stares (and potentially a fine). Wait for green, even at 2 AM with no cars.

Tap water is safe and excellent everywhere — over 1,200 public fountains serve drinkable water. Ask for "Hahnenwasser" at restaurants.

Sunday is quiet — respect the tradition. No lawn mowing, no loud music, no laundry in shared buildings.

US Embassy Denmark: Dag Hammarskjölds Allé 24, 2100 Copenhagen · +45 3341 7100

US Embassy Switzerland: Sulgeneckstrasse 19, 3007 Bern · +41 31 357 7011

Nearest hospital Zürich: Universitätsspital Zürich, Rämistrasse 100. 24-hour emergency department.

Palm Sunday processions may affect some streets in the old town area.

Daylight Saving: Clocks spring forward on Sunday 29 March at 2:00 AM. You lose one hour — double-check your alarm for the flight.

Water: Zürich's tap water is excellent — better than bottled. Over 1,200 public fountains serve drinkable water throughout the city.

Language: Swiss German is the local dialect, but English is widely spoken, especially in hospitality. "Grüezi" (hello) and "Merci vielmal" (thank you) go a long way.

Dress code: Smart casual for all restaurants on this trip. No need for formal attire, but Zürich appreciates looking put-together.

Altitude: Uetliberg is only 871 m — no altitude concerns. Wear sturdy shoes for the summit walk.

Before You Go

Important Information

Entry requirements, immigration updates, and travel advisories for Americans visiting Denmark and Switzerland in March 2026.

Entry Requirements

Americans in the Schengen Area

No visa required for short stays up to 90 days in any 180-day period for both Denmark (EU/Schengen) and Switzerland (Schengen).

Passport validity: Must be valid at least 3 months beyond planned departure date from the Schengen area. The U.S. embassy recommends at least 6 months validity at time of entry.

Sufficient funds & return ticket: Immigration may ask for proof of sufficient funds and a return plane ticket.

90-day Schengen rule: Total stay across ALL Schengen countries cannot exceed 90 days in any 180-day period.

EU Immigration

EU Entry/Exit System (EES)

Launched October 12, 2025.

U.S. citizens will have fingerprints, facial image, passport details, and entry/exit dates collected upon entry to the Schengen area.

No fee required. No advance action needed.

System is being introduced gradually — expect possible queues at immigration on arrival.

Future Requirement

ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorization System)

NOT required for this trip (March 2026).

Scheduled to launch late 2026.

Once active: online pre-travel screening, ~€7 fee, valid 3 years.

Will be required for visa-exempt nationals (including Americans) visiting Schengen countries.

Denmark welcomes American travelers — no restrictions despite Greenland political tensions.

VisitDenmark publicly confirmed Americans remain welcome.

No reports of anti-American sentiment; tourism infrastructure operating normally.
