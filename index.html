Survival Guide

Zürich Card 72 hr covers all trams, buses, S-Bahn, boats on Lake Zürich, the Uetliberg railway, and over 40 museums — including Kunsthaus and Museum Rietberg.

Activate your card in the ZVV app the moment you land at the airport. It starts counting from activation, not purchase.

Home tram stop: Sihlquai/HB — 3 minutes from the hotel. Trams 4, 11, 13, and 17 all stop here.

Zürich HB (main station) is a 5-minute walk. Airport Express departs every 10 minutes, takes 12 minutes to the airport.

Tipping on transport: Not expected. Swiss public transport runs like clockwork.

Palm Sunday (29 March): Most shops will be closed. Restaurants, museums, and public transport run normally.

Saturday shopping: Bahnhofstrasse shops are open until 6 PM on Saturday. Sprüngli, Globus, and Grieder are all on the main strip.

Souvenirs worth buying: Luxemburgerli from Sprüngli, Swiss chocolate from Läderach, a Victorinox knife from the HB shop.

ZVV App — Zürich Card activation and live tram/train schedules.

SBB Mobile — Swiss Federal Railways. Real-time departures, platform info, connections.

Google Maps — Offline maps of Zürich downloaded before departure.

Rejseplanen — Copenhagen public transport (for the stopover).

Currency: Swiss Franc (CHF). Denmark uses Danish Krone (DKK). Neither is the Euro.

Cards accepted everywhere. Visa and Mastercard work in virtually every shop and restaurant. Apple Pay and contactless widely accepted.

Tipping: Not required in Switzerland — service is included. Round up or leave 5–10% for exceptional service if you wish.

ATMs: Available at the airport and throughout the city. Avoid currency conversion at the ATM — always choose to be charged in CHF.

Wi-Fi: Available at both hotels and most cafés/restaurants.

Roaming: Check your carrier's international plan. Switzerland is NOT part of the EU, so EU roaming packages may not apply.

Denmark: EU roaming should work with most US carriers' international plans. Verify before departure.

Emergency: 112 works from any phone, even without a SIM card.

Daylight Saving: Clocks spring forward on Sunday 29 March at 2:00 AM. You lose one hour — double-check your alarm for the flight.

Water: Zürich's tap water is excellent — better than bottled. Over 1,200 public fountains serve drinkable water throughout the city.

Language: Swiss German is the local dialect, but English is widely spoken, especially in hospitality.

Dress code: Smart casual for all restaurants on this trip. No need for formal attire, but Zürich appreciates looking put-together.

Altitude: Uetliberg is only 871 m — no altitude concerns. Wear sturdy shoes for the summit walk.
